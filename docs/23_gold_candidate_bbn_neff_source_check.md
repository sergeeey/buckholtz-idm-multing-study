## Background

### BBN / N_eff Constraint (Standard Cosmology)

**What it is:**
- Big Bang Nucleosynthesis (BBN) occurred at T ~ 1 MeV (first few minutes after Big Bang)
- Light element abundances (D, ³He, ⁴He, ⁷Li) constrain early-universe expansion rate
- Expansion rate depends on total energy density, including relativistic species
- N_eff = effective number of neutrino species (Standard Model predicts N_eff ≈ 3.046)
- Planck 2018 + BBN: N_eff = 2.99 ± 0.17 (consistent with 3 neutrino species)

**Why it matters for dark sectors:**
- If dark sectors contain thermally populated relativistic particles (dark photons, dark neutrinos) at BBN epoch → they contribute to N_eff
- Extra relativistic degrees of freedom → faster expansion → different light element ratios
- Current N_eff constraint allows ΔN_eff ~ 0.2–0.3 (small room for extra species)
- Full mirror sector (exact copy of SM) → ΔN_eff ~ 3 → RULED OUT by BBN (if thermalized)
- Asymmetrically reheated mirror sector (T_dark << T_visible) → may evade BBN

**Key question:**
Does Buckholtz's IDM claim **six isomers / five dark sectors** that are thermally populated at BBN epoch?

If YES → BBN/N_eff is a **high-priority external constraint** (potentially falsifying or requiring asymmetric reheating).  
If NO → BBN/N_eff is **not applicable** (dark isomers are cold, non-relativistic, or structureless).
