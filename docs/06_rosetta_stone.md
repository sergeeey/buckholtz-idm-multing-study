## High-Confidence Mappings (Medium confidence)

These mappings are relatively well-established:

### 1. Isomeric Dark Matter → Hidden Sector
**Mainstream analogue:** Dark sectors with minimal SM coupling

**Why medium confidence:** The concept of dark matter sectors beyond WIMP/axion is mainstream. "Isomeric" structure is the novel part.

**Literature:** Hidden sectors (Strassler & Zurek 2007), mirror matter (Foot et al.), atomic dark matter (Kaplan et al.)

**Caveat:** Exact isomer structure requires clarification.

### 2. MULTING → Modified Gravity
**Mainstream analogue:** Phenomenological modifications to Friedmann equations

**Why medium confidence:** Many modified gravity theories exist (f(R), Galileon, MOND, TeVeS). Multipole-like terms are novel but conceptually similar.

**Literature:** Modified gravity reviews (Clifton et al. 2012, Joyce et al. 2015)

**Caveat:** Exact functional form unknown, so detailed comparison not possible.

### 3. Direct Outcomes → Goal-Oriented Problem-Solving
**Mainstream analogue:** Backwards induction, outcome-driven reasoning

**Why medium confidence:** Methodology, not physics content. Many similar frameworks in decision theory.

**Literature:** Decision analysis, operations research

**Caveat:** Not a physics claim, orthogonal to IDM/MULTING physics content.

## Low-Confidence Mappings (requires clarification)

These mappings are speculative:

### 1. Dipole repulsion → Preferred-frame effect
**Why low confidence:** GR is frame-independent. Dipole terms suggest preferred-frame effects, which are constrained by PPN tests.

**Mainstream concern:** Solar System tests constrain preferred-frame effects to < 10^-4 level (Cassini).

**Action required:** Map MULTING dipole to PPN formalism and check constraints.

### 2. Quadrupole attraction → Higher-order metric terms
**Why low confidence:** Many possibilities (tidal effects, Yukawa modifications, higher-curvature terms). Cannot map without functional form.

**Action required:** Request explicit H(z, beta_d, beta_q) formula.

### 3. Beta parameters → Phenomenological fit parameters
**Why low confidence:** Could be: (1) fitted, (2) derived, (3) normalization choice. Cannot map without clarification.

**Action required:** Clarify beta derivation vs fitting.
