## 5. Derivation check
- Equal-density-per-isomer: **NOT derived** (no isomer-level baryogenesis / thermal history anywhere in paper)
- The "+" excess: Sec 4.5 lists 4 speculative options (axions; reach-6 early energy outflow; non-decaying neutral baryon analogs; neutrino-analog density) — **qualitative, no quantitative derivation**
- Author acknowledges the gap by construction: the claim is written as "(5+):1", with the plus carrying the unexplained excess

Sanity note: even all-neutron-like dark baryons add only ~+0.08% mass density per isomer (m_n − m_p − m_e = 0.78 MeV) — cannot close a 7% gap.

## 6. Alternative explanations / base-rate risk
Small-integer coincidence base rate is high: observed 5.36 sits near 5 (−7%), 16/3 (−0.6%), 27/5 (+0.7%)... Many small fractions land within a few percent of any O(5) number. Integer-counting proximity alone therefore carries weak evidential weight — exactly why a derivation (equal density per isomer + thermal history) is the load-bearing missing piece.

## 7. Interpretation
**Approximate post-hoc integer-counting match.** Not a physical prediction (no mechanism fixes equal density per isomer); not blocked (sources located); not a refutation (the "(5+):1" phrasing is deliberately elastic and the counting basis remains a candidate).

## 8. Verdict
**PARTIAL** — integer ratio is in the right neighborhood (−7%), but the derivation/thermal history is missing, literal 5.0 is 5.6σ from the Planck cross-check, and the excess is absorbed into unquantified "pluses".

## Overclaim guard
